Here are some resources for further exploration about :

  • Swift was developed by Apple to update its Objective-C language (which dates to the 1980s). As an alternative, Swift is simpler in its syntax than Objective-C in the same way that jQuery is simpler than vanilla JavaScript.
  • Some say that Swift is one of the fastest growing programming languages. That's because most iOS apps were written in Objective-C, a now-dated language that Swift replaced. If you want to program mobile apps, Swift is probably your best bet.
  • See what the dev community is saying about Swift on Reddit.
  • Most applications for Swift are, unsurprisingly, part of the iOS mobile app ecosystem. They include LinkedIn, Firefox, Wordpress, Asana, Wikipedia, Lyft, Sky Guide, and Kickstarter. Website development in Swift is uncommon, but technically possible, and such applications could become much more widely used with Apple's new, advanced chipset in its line of Mac computers
